Data Narrative · What the Analysis Shows
The Pricing Inflection

The Q4 2025 pricing revision was designed to close the value gap with enterprise competitors. Revenue data confirms it succeeded — but only for deals above $100K. Below that threshold, the revision introduced friction that accelerated churn in cohorts acquired after October 2025.

−11ppSMB renewal rate decline · Oct 2025 cohort vs prior year average
Cohort Health Signal

Jan 2026 cohort M2 retention of 94% is the system's clearest warning signal. Historical data shows cohorts that dip below 95% at M2 have a 68% probability of churning before M12. At current cohort size, this represents approximately $340K of ARR at risk.

Customer Success flagged onboarding friction in three Jan 2026 accounts — this matches the M2 dip timing precisely and should be treated as causally related, not coincidental.

The recommended intervention is a 30-day SMB onboarding review targeting sub-$25K accounts closed after October 2025. Based on prior recovery rates, a successful intervention could recover 60–70% of at-risk ARR.
